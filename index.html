Sergei Tikhonov

I'm a first-year PhD student at UT Austin.

I aim to build effective algorithms by simplifying complexity and bringing the core mathematics into focus. More generally, I explore the algorithmic bedrock of machine learning — places where sharper math leads to new tools.

My research interests include

  • Statistical and Computational Learning Theory
  • Flow and Diffusion models for Generative Modeling
  • Probabilistic Graphical Modeling and Variational Inference
  • Minimax and Le Cam Theory

Previously, I earned my master's degree at the University of Chicago and bachelor's degree at the HSE university in Moscow. At UChicago, I had the privilage of collaborating with Aaron Schein on probabilistic graphical models.

I also spent 3 beautiful months in Optiver, working with the Head of Quantitative Research Artur Swiech on speeding up the numerical algorithms.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing chess and travelling around the world.
